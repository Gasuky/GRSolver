About

I am an independent inventor and technology solver based in the Scottish Highlands. I hold two granted UK patents developed entirely through independent research — without institutional affiliation, laboratory access, or prior funding.

My approach is built on first-principles reasoning and cross-domain thinking. I look for problems where the solution already exists in a different field, and where connecting those fields produces something genuinely new.

I am not a company or academic institution. I work for Royal Mail and as a self-employed painter. Innovation is what I do with the time and energy I have left — and that constraint has never stopped me from getting to a granted patent.

How I Work

  • Define the real problem, not the stated one
  • Map what already exists and why it fails
  • Identify the constraint that everyone has accepted
  • Search adjacent fields for mechanisms that remove it
  • Build the minimum concept that proves the connection is valid
  • Stress-test it against the strongest objection I can find

Granted Patents

GB2615187.8

CNT-NEMS Acoustic Switch Architecture

Carbon nanotube nanoelectromechanical switches activated by bulk acoustic waves propagating through a silicon substrate via an aluminium nitride piezoelectric layer. The architecture enables zero-leakage power gating in integrated circuits — eliminating static current loss across entire logic domains without conventional control wiring — and opens new directions in physical hardware identity primitives.

UK Intellectual Property Office

GB2614957.5

Molecular Dielectric Interface for Piezoelectric-Semiconductor Integration

A self-assembled monolayer derived from functionalised perfluorinated compounds, positioned between a crystalline alpha-quartz substrate and an active silicon logic layer. The interface provides simultaneous electrical isolation, thermal protection below the 573 °C quartz degradation threshold, and lossless acoustic transmission of piezoelectric clock signals directly into the transistor matrix — resolving two previously insurmountable barriers to integrating on-chip quartz oscillators.

Hardware Security · Active

Physical Identity Primitive Based on Patented Nanomechanical Architecture

Investigating a new class of physically unclonable function derived from the nanomechanical properties inherent to the architecture in patent GB2615187.8. The approach exploits atomic-scale fabrication variability as a cryptographic entropy source — producing device identities that are grounded in quantum mechanics rather than computational assumptions, and that register physical tampering irreversibly in the material itself. Proposal submitted to ARIA Trust Everything, Everywhere 2026.
